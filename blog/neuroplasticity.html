The principles of Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is the phenomenon where neurons in our brain change and reorganise as we learn and ‘do’ overtime. Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity enables people to recognise that they fundamentally can change and learn new information on a biological level.

Engaging with Neuroplasticity for your benifit

Neuroplasticity also teaches us that repetitive practice strengthens the connections between neurons, thereby making you better / stronger at whatever you are learning. An individual might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity when learning new skills such as a musical instrument, by engaging principles such as “Practice makes perfect” an individual might be motivated to continue practising their new skill, resulting in improvement.

How do you increase your neuroplasticity?

Practice, Practice, Practice! Neuroplasticity is the ability for neurons to rewire over time, this is constantly happening, but can be sped up by continuing the same skill as often as possible!

Why is a Growth Mindset relevant?

Having a strong growth mindset can speed up your learning process exponentially. It relates to increased motivation, increased focus, and increased memory related to learning. This obviously makes it very relevant during study at Dev Academy, because all of these traits will have a strong positive impact on your learning.
