Te Whare Tapu Wha Wellbeing Plan

Te Whare Tapu Wha Maori wellbeing model created in 1984. It provides a unique perspective on health using 4 pillars or walls that hold up the whare of wellbeing. These pillars are;

    • Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)
      Taha Hinengaro (emotional and mental wellbeing)
      Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
      Taha Whﾄ］au (friends & family wellbeing)

    • taha tinana (physical wellbeing)

    Asside from the obvious benifits of keeping a good physical health, I find that when I exercise, I sleep better and feel happier during the day. So far, I have made sure to keep a good phsyical health by exercising at the gym each day in the afternoon. This acts as a good way to take a break from my study, and a way of keeping fit. I often go for walks in the evening as well before bed.

    Taha Hinengaro (emotional and mental wellbeing)

    With regards to my emotional and mental wellbeing, I find that I often feel happier when my other pillars of wellbeing are upheld especially when it comes to Taha Whﾄ］au (friends & family wellbeing). I'm a very extroverted person, and find just interacting with people I am similar to be something the postivly effects my mental health.

    Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

    I'm a very un-spiritual person in general. My biggest weakness found by a personality test I took in Sprint 1 (or 2?) was infact spiritualily. I'm non religious, but I do have a strong interest in understanding I guess you could say 'spiritual questions.' (Meaning of life, what is counciousness, etc...). I guess this interest could fall into Taha Wairua, but I don't see it as an important aspect of my wellbeing.

    Taha Whﾄ］au (family wellbeing)

    Taha Whﾄ］au is one of the pillars I am more focussed on when it comes to my wellbeing. Given that Dev Academy is online, there isn't as many aspects of my life at the moment where my social battery gets fullfillment! At the moment, I really try to make plans with mates over the weekeneds, and often host get-togeathers with friends. I don't see much changing over my time during foundations, but I look foward to the in-person bootcamp where I can make some more connections with people.
